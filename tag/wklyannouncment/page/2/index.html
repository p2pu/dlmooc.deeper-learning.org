Wk. 4 email – Personalization, student voice and choice

Posted on February 9, 2014 by Karen Fasimpaur
Reply

(This is a duplicate of the Feb. 9 email sent to registered participants.)

Dear DLMOOC participants,

It’s week 4 of DLMOOC! This week we’re looking at personalized learning, student voice and choice. All the resources and activities for this week are available here.

  • This week, our suggested resources include a video that shows how Edvisions schools co-plan projects with students and an article from Pedro Noguera about asking our students how to improve our schools.
  • Our panel discussion this Monday, Feb. 10 at 4pm Pacific (Los Angeles) will feature Keven Kroehler, Executive Director, Edvisions Schools; Kathleen Cushman, journalist and author of Fires in the Mind; Brandon Wiley, Executive Director, Asia Society; Alison Cook-Sather, Professor of Education, Bryn Mawr College, and editor of “Teaching and Learning Together in Higher Education”; and Samantha Alander, a student from the Minnesota New Country School.
  • Our “Lens in the Classroom” live event on Thursday, Feb. 13 at 4pm Pacific (Los Angeles) will feature an in depth discussion with students about what deeper learning means to them.
  • We hope you’ll share your ideas about bringing learner voice and choice into our learning environments. Please post your experiences in our G+ community or add them to the Deeper Learning Story Bank.

We also want to make you aware of the Deeper Learning conference, which will be on March 26-28 at High Tech High in San Diego, California. You can find out more about this premier international event here. Let us know if you’ll be there, so we can organize a DLMOOC meet-up!

Sincerely,

Ben, Rob, Laura, Ryan, Karen, and the whole DLMOOC team

Week 2 email

Posted on January 26, 2014 by Karen Fasimpaur
Reply

(This is a duplicate of the Jan. 26 email sent to registered participants.)

Dear DLMOOC community,

We hope you had as much fun last week as we did! And if you’re just joining us, jump right in! DLMOOC is set up so that folks can join in progress and take part in a variety of ways. It’s easy to get overwhelmed by so much goodness, but just dip your toes in a way that’s manageable for you.

This week we’ll be talking about student work and deeper learning and taking a look at some of the things going on at Expeditionary Learning (EL) schools. Here are some highlights for the week, and a full list of everything can be found here.

  • We’ll be having another great panel discussion this Monday, Jan. 27 at 4pm Pacific (Los Angeles). (Throughout the rest of the course, panel discussions will be on Mondays at this same time, and they will all be archived for later viewing.) This one will feature Ron Berger and others talking about student work.
  • Our first “Lens in the Classroom” live event will be held this Thursday, Jan. 30 at 4pm Pacific (Los Angeles). In this, you’ll have a “fishbowl” view of a protocol looking at a teacher’s real dilemma of how to think about student work in his classroom.
  • There are some excellent resources from Ron Berger and EL this week including “Crafting Beautiful Work,” “Highlighting Student Work,” and the “Austin’s Butterfly” video.
  • You’ll have several chances to reflect on student work yourself, including this week’s Tweet of the Week and Put it Into Practice.
  • As always, feel free to alter these activities to meet your own learning needs, and keep up those conversations in your groups and in the broader community.
  • If you tuned in on Thursday, you know that we had some tech challenges. We are working on addressing server issues but will have a workaround in place for Monday that we hope will enable a successful panel presentation. We will likely not have chat working directly on the site and encourage you to use Twitter for backchannel conversation during the panel conversation.

Keep those comments, questions, and suggestions coming! That’s how we all learn.

Sincerely,

Ben, Rob, Laura, Ryan, Karen, and the whole DLMOOC team

Week 1 email #2

Posted on January 23, 2014 by Karen Fasimpaur
2

(This is a duplicate of the Jan. 23 email sent to registered participants.)

Dear DLMOOC participants,

What a great first week of DLMOOC we’re having! Groups are forming; conversations are happening; the community is super engaged!

Community members have been doing things like setting up a participant map that we can all add ourselves to, setting up Vialogues to discuss videos, and adding their blogs to our new blog hub. We love it, and hope you will feel free to come up with more exciting ways to make DLMOOC even better.

You can visit this page for a detailed list of all the readings, videos and other activities for this week. Don’t forget our panel discussion at 4pm Pacific today. (This will also be archived at www.dlmooc.net/live.)

As we’re getting started, we want to make a big class photo for this course. Follow the link in the email to add your photo and see others in the course.

You might also want to consider doing one or more of the following:

  • Tweet of the Week - Think of a time during your youth when you had a significant learning experience. What was it + why was it so significant?
    Tweet your response with the hashtags #WhatisDL #dlmooc
  • Put it Into Practice - These are activity ideas you can do individually or as part of a group.
  • Comment on others’ posts on the G+ community, Twitter, or on blogs.

And if all this is new and mysterious to you and you’re lurking around to see what’s going on, that’s great! We want you to participate in whatever way works for you.

See you next week when we’ll be looking at student work for deeper learning.

Sincerely,

Ben, Rob, Laura, Ryan, Karen, and the whole DLMOOC team

Week 1 email

Posted on January 19, 2014 by Karen Fasimpaur
2

(This is a duplicate of the Jan. 12 email sent to registered participants.)

Dear DLMOOC-ers,

Welcome to week 1, where we’ll get to know each other and start looking at deeper learning — what is it, why we want it, and how it promotes student engagement and achievement.

We have some exciting things going on this week. Please feel free to participate in any or all of these activities, on our schedule or on some other schedule that best fits your life. It’s your MOOC — make the most of it!

All of this week’s activities, readings and videos are posted on the web site; here are a few highlights:

  • Thurs., Jan. 23, 4pm Pacific – Panel discussion of “What is deeper learning and why does it matter?” with Eduardo Briceño, Mindset Works; Marc Chun, Hewlett Foundation; Camille Farrington, University of Chicago; Jal Mehta, Harvard Graduate School of Education, and Larry Rosenstock, High Tech High.
    This session will be streamed live and an archive will also be posted.
  • Think about forming or joining a smaller group, either face to face or virtual, to explore DLMOOC with; more information here.
  • A few suggested readings:
    • Larry Rosenstock and Rob Riordan make the case for project based learning
    • Eduardo Briceño on mindsets, student agency, and deeper learning
    • Jal Mehta on deeper learning and the common core
    • An infographic on deeper learning from Tom Vander Ark and the Getting Smart team

Make sure you’ve joined the DLMOOC G+ community — that’s where most of the action is going on. You can post your reflections there as well.

If there is anything we can do to make your learning experience better, please let us know.

Sincerely,

Ben, Rob, Laura, Ryan, Karen, and the whole DLMOOC team
