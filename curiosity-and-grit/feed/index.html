Thank you so much for sharing this beautiful DL story!

As I was reading your piece, I was reminded of a quote from J.D. Salinger’s “Franny and Zooey”:

“Seymour once said to me – in a crosstown bus, of all places – that all legitimate religious study must lead to unlearning the differences, the illusory differences, between boys and girls, animals and stones, day and night, heat and cold.”

Although this quote is in direct reference to religion and spirituality, I always see a connection to the notion of “unlearning the differences” and our students (I teach 12th grade) dwindling sense of wonder and creativity. What are the “differences” that our students see and how do those “differences” keep them from diving freely into their own sense of wonder and creativity?

Also, I LOVED the bridge you built between creativity and grit. Forget it’s relevance to our students…that’s a great reminder for me in my own existence!

Thanks again for sharing!
