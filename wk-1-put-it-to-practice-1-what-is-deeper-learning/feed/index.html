]]> By: Ivan Diego http://dlmooc.deeper-learning.org/wk-1-put-it-to-practice-1-what-is-deeper-learning/#comment-5394 Fri, 24 Jan 2014 07:35:13 +0000 http://dlmooc.deeper-learning.org/?p=405#comment-5394 Although I tend to agree with the overall picture, I think we should sober up on the hyper-relevant curriculum imperative. Abraham Flexner’s “The usefulness of useless knowledge” would make a great counterbalancing argument.

“In The Usefulness of Useless Knowledge (PDF), originally published in the October 1939 issue of Harper’s, American educator Abraham Flexner explores this dangerous tendency to forgo pure curiosity in favor of pragmatism — in science, in education, and in human thought at large — to deliver a poignant critique of the motives encouraged in young minds, contrasting those with the drivers that motivated some of history’s most landmark discoveries.”

Upfront pragmatism (whatever its source, educ authorities, teachers or kids from the hood) may not be the best road to follow. The recipe, if there’s one, needs far more ingredients eg. a dash of idleness to engage in daydreaming perhaps?
