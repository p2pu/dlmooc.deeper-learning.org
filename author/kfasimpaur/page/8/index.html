DLMOOC Preview – Put It Into Practice

Posted on November 6, 2013 by Karen Fasimpaur
Reply

The DLMOOC Put it Into Practice (PIP) activities are a way for you to explore what our topics in more depth with students or colleagues. We’d also invite you to share what you did and how it went with a small group at your site or on our G+ Community.

In this week’s activity, you and/or your students or colleagues will have a chance to assess your own mindset and think more about how academic mindsets affect our learning.

  1. Take of one of these academic mindset assessments (You might also have your students or colleagues take one and then debrief as a group):

  • Test your mindset from Carol Dweck
  • What’s my mindset? from Mindset Works (ages 12 and up)
  • What’s my classroom mindset? from Mindset Works (educator oriented)
  • What’s my school’s mindset? from Mindset Works (educator oriented)
  • The Mindset Self- Assessment from Global Innovations (career oriented)

Or if you’d prefer, try developing your mindset survey and post it to the G+ Community. SurveyMonkey or Google doc forms are good tools for something like this.

  1. Look at the results. Do you mostly have a fixed or growth mindset? Does this seem right to you? How do you think this affects your learning process and how you approach things in your daily life?

  2. Post a short comment on the G+ Community about this exercise and what you learned from it in the context of our discussions this week, and/or tweet it out as well. Don’t forget our hashtags: #dlmooc and #deeperlearning.

11/1/2013 – Welcome to DLMOOC preview!

Posted on November 1, 2013 by Karen Fasimpaur
Reply

Welcome to the Deeper Learning MOOC (DLMOOC) preview week!

We are starting on Monday, November 4, and we’ll be looking at Academic Mindsets. We know you are participating in this MOOC because you want to grow and change. That’s what Academic Mindsets are all about — the motivational forces that influence engagement in deeper learning.

We have some exciting things going on this week. Please feel free to participate in any or all of these activities, on our schedule or on some other schedule that best fits your life. It’s your MOOC — make the most of it!

  • On-going discussions in our DLMOOC G+ Community
  • Live web-based discussions (You can view these live here or view the archives later.)
    • Mon., November 4 at 4pm Pacific: Academic Mindset Panel
    • Wed., November 6 at 4pm Pacific: Tuning Protocol on Academic Mindsets
  • Readings
    • Mindsets and Student Agency by Eduardo Briceño
    • The Effort Effect by Marina Krakovsky
    • Fixed Mind-set vs. Growth Mind-set
  • Videos
    • Carol Dweck: The Effect of Praise on Mindsets
    • Angela Lee Duckworth: The key to success? Grit
  • Tweet of the Week – Describe something that you have (or used to have) a fixed mindset about and why. Tweet with the hashtags #fixedmindset #dlmooc
  • Put It into Practice - An activity with students or colleagues that explicitly addresses the issue of academic mindsets. You will share with a small group on what you did and how it went.

More details on all this, as well as additional resources, can be found here.

Please also join our DLMOOC G+ Community, where we’ll be discussing all this and more.

Happy MOOC’ing, and again we are happy to have you join our preview week. If there is anything we can do to make your learning experience better, please let us know.

Sincerely,

Ben, Rob, Laura, Ryan, Karen, and the whole DLMOOC team
